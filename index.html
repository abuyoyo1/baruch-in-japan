🗾 Japan — The Big Picture
General facts about the country you're visiting
🚅
World Record
The Shinkansen has never had a fatal accident in 60 years
Since 1964, Japan's bullet train has carried over 10 billion passengers without a single fatality from a crash. Average delay: under 1 minute. You'll ride it multiple times on this trip.
🏪
Only in Japan
4 million vending machines — one for every 30 people
They dispense not just drinks but hot meals, umbrellas, fresh eggs, underwear, and live crabs. In Tokyo you'll pass dozens every day.
🍣
Food
Tokyo has more Michelin stars than any city in the world
Over 200 starred restaurants — more than Paris and New York combined. A ¥600 ramen can be as extraordinary as a ¥30,000 kaiseki. You'll experience both on this trip.
🗼
History
Tokyo was nearly destroyed twice in 50 years — and rebuilt both times
The 1923 earthquake and 1945 firebombing each leveled much of the city. The city you're visiting is almost entirely built after 1945.
😴
Only in Japan
Sleeping on the job can be a sign of dedication
Inemuri — sleeping in public during the work day — is considered proof you worked so hard you're exhausted. You'll see salarymen asleep on the Shinkansen. Completely acceptable.

✈️ TLV → DXB → NRT
✈️
History
Israel and Japan share a surprising post-war parallel
Both nations rebuilt from near-zero within a generation. Japan is Israel's largest trading partner in Asia. Tonight's flight connects two of the world's most technologically advanced small nations.
🇦🇪
Record
Dubai Airport Terminal 3 is the largest building in the world by floor area
Dubai International handles over 87 million passengers a year. Terminal 3 covers 1.7 million square metres. Your 2-hour layover barely scratches the surface.
🌏
Culture
Many Israelis feel an immediate cultural resonance in Japan
Orderliness, intensity, compulsory military service, family loyalty — the two cultures share more than meets the eye, despite being 9,200 km apart.
Jul 12 — Tokyo (Arrival)
🏙️
History
Tokyo was a fishing village called Edo until 1868
The world's largest metro area — 37 million people — grew from a small estuary settlement in under 160 years.
🚆
Record
The N'EX runs to the second
JR East issues formal apology letters if a train is more than 5 minutes late. Station clocks are synchronized to the second. Welcome to the world's most time-precise country.
🏘️
History
Kyobashi was once Tokyo's most elegant merchant district
The first bridge built after Edo Castle's construction. Today a quiet business district between Ginza and Tokyo Station — an ideal base most tourists overlook entirely.
Jul 13 — Takayama
🏯
History
Isolated by mountains, Takayama developed its own distinct culture
The Sanmachi Suji merchant quarter looks almost exactly as it did in the Edo period — no other Japanese city has preserved its old town this completely.
🏛️
History
Takayama Jinya is the only surviving Edo-period government outpost in Japan
Functionally active from 1692 to 1969 — nearly 300 years. The interrogation room and torture chamber in the basement are sobering.
🍶
Food
Takayama sake is exceptional because of its water
The Japanese Alps filter snowmelt through granite for decades before it emerges as ultra-pure spring water. When you see a cedar ball (sugidama) above a door, that's a brewery.
Jul 14 — Kamikochi
🚗
Only in Japan
Private cars have been banned from Kamikochi since 1975
Despite 1.5 million visitors a year, the valley remains remarkably peaceful. The ban is now studied worldwide as a model for protecting fragile natural sites.
🌋
Nature
Taisho Pond was created by a volcanic eruption in 1915
Mount Yake — one of Japan's few active volcanoes — erupted and dammed the Azusa River. The dead tree stumps still visible in the water are the original forest, drowned over 100 years ago.
🧗
History
Kamikochi was "discovered" by a British missionary in 1891
Reverend Walter Weston wrote a book that triggered Japan's mountaineering boom. A bronze relief near Kappa-bashi honors him — look for it as you cross the bridge.
Jul 15 — Shirakawa-go → Kanazawa
🏠
History
Shirakawa-go's roofs are built without nails — and rebuilt by the whole village
Only rope and wooden pegs. A single roof replacement (every 30–40 years) involves 200+ villagers working for one day in a tradition called yui.
🥷
History
Shirakawa-go may have been a ninja training ground
Local legend holds the remote valley was used by the Shirakawa clan. Whether true or not, Kanazawa's Ninja Temple was built with 29 staircases across 7 floors — hiding in a 2-story shell.
🥇
Record
Kanazawa produces 99% of all gold leaf made in Japan
One gram of gold beaten into sheets covering 1 square metre. The gold leaf on your ice cream tonight is almost certainly made within a few kilometres of where you're standing.
Jul 16 — Kanazawa → Kyoto
💣
History
Kyoto was removed from the atomic bomb list by one man's honeymoon memory
US Secretary of War Henry Stimson personally removed Kyoto from the target list, having visited on his honeymoon. Hiroshima and Nagasaki were bombed instead.
🎏
History
The Gion Matsuri started as a plague-prevention ritual in 869 AD
1,157 years old and still running. The yamahoko floats weigh up to 12 tonnes and are assembled without a single nail.
🏯
History
The Ninja Temple has 29 staircases across 7 floors — but looks like a 2-story house
Hidden corridors, trapdoor rooms, a well connecting to an underground escape tunnel, and a pit trap for assassins. No actual ninja ever lived here — the complexity itself was the defense.
Jul 17 — Kyoto (Grand Parade + Arashiyama)
🎋
Nature
The Arashiyama bamboo grows 90cm per day
Moso bamboo is the fastest-growing plant on Earth. The grove's acoustic quality — thousands of hollow stalks clicking in the wind — was designated one of Japan's "100 Soundscapes to Preserve."
🐒
Nature
The Arashiyama monkeys remember human faces — for years
Japanese macaques recognize and remember individual human faces for years. They're also one of the only non-human primates known to wash food before eating.
🎭
Culture
Geisha in Kyoto are called Geiko — and train for 5+ years before their debut
Spot a maiko tonight by her longer hanging obi sash and elaborate hair ornaments. The Gion district is one of only a few remaining hanamachi (flower towns) left in Japan.
Jul 18 — Kyoto (Culture Day)
⛩️
Record
Fushimi Inari's 10,000 torii gates were each donated by a business praying for profit
Each gate has the donor's name on the back. Gates cost ¥400,000–¥1,000,000 depending on size. At 6:30am as planned, the gates emerge from mist with almost nobody there.
☕
Culture
The tea ceremony's founding master was ordered to commit ritual suicide
Sen no Rikyu codified chado in the 16th century. He was eventually ordered to commit seppuku by the shogun for becoming too powerful. He first performed one final, perfect tea ceremony.
🔥
History
Kinkaku-ji was burned down by a student monk in 1950 — and inspired a Mishima novel
A 22-year-old novice set fire to the original. Author Yukio Mishima wrote a novel — "The Temple of the Golden Pavilion" — exploring obsession with beauty and destruction. The pavilion today has 5x more gold leaf than the original.
Jul 19 — Kyoto → Tokyo
🚥
Record
Shibuya Crossing: 3,000 people per green light, half a million per day
Entirely self-organizing — no barriers, no marshals, almost never a collision. From Shibuya Sky above, it looks like a living organism.
⛩️
History
Yasaka Shrine has been operating continuously for 1,350 years
Founded in 656 AD. It's the origin point of the Gion Matsuri — all floats begin and end here. Open 24 hours.
👗
Culture
Shibuya is where global youth culture is invented — in real time
Designers Rei Kawakubo, Yohji Yamamoto, and Issey Miyake all trace their roots here. Trends cycle in weeks, not seasons.
Jul 20 — Tokyo Day 1
⛩️
History
Senso-ji's golden statue has never been seen by anyone
According to legend, two fishermen pulled it from the Sumida River in 628 AD. It remains hidden inside the inner sanctum to this day. Nobody alive has ever seen it. 30 million visitors a year come anyway.
🎮
History
Akihabara was a black market for electrical parts after WWII
It evolved into Japan's electronics hub, then the center of anime and gaming culture. "Otaku" culture born here is now a $24 billion global industry.
👗
Only in Japan
Takeshita Street is 350 metres long — and shaped global fashion
Where Decora, Lolita, and Visual Kei were born. It famously inspired a Gwen Stefani album. Its energy comes from treating fashion as radical self-expression rather than commerce.
Jul 21 — Tokyo Day 2
🐟
Record
A single bluefin tuna at Tsukiji once sold for $3.1 million
In January 2019, a 278kg tuna sold for ¥333 million — roughly $11,000 per kilogram. The tuna you'll eat for breakfast will cost significantly less.
🎨
World Record
TeamLab Planets holds a Guinness World Record
Most visited museum by a single art group — 2.5 million visitors in one year. Remember: it's barefoot and involves wading through water. Wear easy shoes.
🚗
Culture
JDM cars became global legends partly because they were illegal to import
The Nissan Skyline GT-R, Toyota Supra, Honda NSX — sold exclusively in Japan, then immortalized by Gran Turismo and Fast & Furious. Daikoku PA is where they gather on weekend nights.
Jul 22 — Tokyo Day 3
🤼
History
Sumo is a 1,500-year-old Shinto ritual — not just a sport
The ring (dohyo) is consecrated ground blessed by a Shinto priest. The salt thrown before each bout is a purification ritual. A wrestler's entire life is governed by tradition.
🎵
Culture
Shimokitazawa was nearly destroyed by a highway — and fought back for a decade
Residents launched an extraordinary protest that eventually forced the road underground. The streets you'll wander exist because of one of Japan's most successful grassroots urban campaigns.
🍺
Only in Japan
Golden Gai's 200 bars survived Tokyo's redevelopment through sheer stubbornness
Developers, yakuza intimidation, and arson attempts all failed to move the bar owners. The result: 200 bars in an area the size of a supermarket car park, on some of the world's most valuable land.

History
Haneda opened in 1931 — and was seized by the US military after WWII
Returned to Japan in 1952. Terminal 3, where you depart tonight, opened in 2010.
🎁
Culture
Omiyage — you must bring gifts from every trip, for everyone
A social obligation so deeply ingrained that every airport sells beautifully packaged regional specialties for exactly this purpose. Budget ¥5,000–10,000 per person. The people back home are counting on you.
🌅
Culture
Japan has a word for the bittersweet feeling of a journey ending
Mono no aware (物の哀れ) — the gentle sadness of transience. The feeling of a beloved place disappearing in an airport window. As your flight lifts from Haneda tonight, you'll probably understand exactly what it means.
💴
Cash
Japan remains largely cash-based. Withdraw at 7-Eleven or Japan Post ATMs. Shirakawa-go and Kamikochi are largely card-free.
🎫
JR Pass
14-day JR Pass covers N'EX, Hida Limited Express, Hikari Shinkansen, Thunderbird. Does NOT cover Nozomi — use Hikari or pay separately.
🌡️
July heat
Tokyo and Kyoto average 33–36°C with high humidity. Carry a folding fan, stay hydrated, wear light clothing. Kamikochi at 1,500m will feel like a blessing.
📱
Connectivity
Pocket WiFi or SIM card essential for navigation. Book via Ninja WiFi or IIJmio before departure.
👟
Shoes
You'll walk 15–20km/day. Comfortable sneakers are non-negotiable. For temples: shoes easy to slip on and off.
🎒
Luggage forwarding
Ship bags between cities via takuhaibin (~¥2,000/bag). Ask at any hotel front desk. Huge convenience on the Tokyo→Takayama→Kyoto legs.
